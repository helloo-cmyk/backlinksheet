### KEY CHARACTERISTICS SECTION
**[Eyebrow]** Traits
**[H2]** Key Characteristics of Brat Typography
**[p]** The brat font style is defined by six consistent design principles.
- **[H3] Bold weight:** **[p]** Heavy strokes at 700+ weight. The text feels dense and grounded, not thin or decorative. This is what separates brat typography from standard body text.
- **[H3] Clean sans-serif forms:** **[p]** No serifs, no swashes, no ornamentation. The letterforms follow geometric sans-serif construction with even stroke widths and open counters.
- **[H3] Tight letter spacing:** **[p]** Characters sit close together with deliberate, compact gaps. Tight letter spacing gives the text a solid, connected appearance that reads as a single visual block.
- **[H3] High contrast:** **[p]** Dark text on bright backgrounds or light text on deep black. The brat typography aesthetic depends on strong separation between foreground and background.
- **[H3] Minimal ornamentation:** **[p]** The text is the design. No gradients, no shadows, no outlines. Just type on a flat color field.
- **[H3] Centered or deliberate layouts:** **[p]** Text is placed with intention. Centered alignment is most common, but offset compositions work when the asymmetry is clearly purposeful.
